The three tiers of governed intelligence

NSI, GDI, and PGI are proprietary tiers of governed intelligence invented by DeBacco Nexus and defined in our patent filings. They appear across NODO, AIRS Nexus, and the Inference Governance Module (IGM), giving institutions a repeatable pattern for governed AI.

NSI — Negative Space Intelligence

NSI is the audit layer that asks a harder question than “What data do we have?” It asks, “What can this schema not see by design?” NSI scans structures, relationships, and expected fields to surface blindness zones — places where an institution has no visibility even though a domain expert would expect it.

  • Identifies structural gaps and cross‑agency blind spots.
  • Flags missing temporal and causal links in your data.
  • Turns omissions into tracked governance objects.
  • Provides the map that NODO and AIRS build on.

GDI — Governed Data Intelligence

GDI is the deterministic layer that runs on governed data and NSI findings. It encodes zero‑token governance rules that fire automatically whenever relevant records are touched, turning high‑level policy into executable constraints.

  • Expresses junction logic in explicit IF / AND / THEN rules.
  • Runs checks every time data is viewed or analyzed.
  • Uses zero AI tokens for facts that structure already governs.
  • Logs every rule fire into the governance receipt trail.

PGI — Predictive Governed Intelligence

PGI is the predictive layer that only activates inside the envelope defined by NSI and GDI. It uses AI models to generate bounded predictions with confidence levels, intervention windows, and human approval queues, under explicit pre‑inference constraints.

  • Produces outcome probabilities with confidence bounds.
  • Suggests intervention windows and recommended actions.
  • Operates under token budgets set by the IGM.
  • Emits receipts showing what data was used and why.

The governed systems we ship

Our products are not demos. They are deployed systems that run in live institutional environments and answer to real accountability demands.

NODO — Native Orthogonal Data Output

NODO is a governed AI architecture for institutional interoperability. It reveals cross‑schema blindness zones, generates deterministic rules at the interoperability knot, and governs cross‑agency inference before any model is allowed to answer.

  • Maps where separate schemas are collectively blind.
  • Creates junction fields for invisible interactions.
  • Imposes pre‑inference governance gates at the knot.
  • Logs receipts for every cross‑schema traversal.

AIRS Nexus — Adaptive Intelligent Reporting System

AIRS Nexus turns domains like justice‑involved veterans into governed applications with the NSI / GDI / PGI pattern: NSI to identify schema gaps, GDI to fire zero‑token deterministic rules, and PGI to produce bounded predictions with human approval queues.

  • Every record, field, and change is governed from birth.
  • NSI surfaces structural gaps your schema cannot see.
  • GDI runs deterministic rules with zero wasted tokens.
  • PGI predictions arrive with confidence and receipts.

IGM — Inference Governance Module

The IGM is the patented inference governance engine that sits inside our architectures and systems. It constrains behavior, budgets power and tokens, and produces auditable receipts for every interaction with an AI model.

  • Token and power‑aware budgeting for every inference.
  • Pre‑defined governance modes and escalation paths.
  • Deterministic guardrails around probabilistic models.
  • Receipts that prove what the system did and why.

Founded from the front lines of systems failure

DeBacco Nexus was founded by James DeBacco, a Marine Corps veteran, MSW social worker, and Doctor of Social Work (DSW) candidate at the USC Suzanne Dworak-Peck School of Social Work. James serves as a Veterans Service Representative and Veterans Treatment Court liaison for Los Angeles County and is an advocate and partner with CalCompute, where he collaborates on governed AI research and infrastructure.

Working inside fragmented institutions, James saw the same pattern everywhere: separate systems, separate schemas, and no governed way to see what they were collectively blind to. DeBacco Nexus exists to solve that problem as infrastructure. NODO, AIRS Nexus, and the IGM are the first expressions of that work.
